Jimmy Page

The Legendary Guitarist of Led Zeppelin

Jimmy Page is a legendary guitarist and founder of Led Zeppelin. Known for his innovative playing and iconic riffs, he helped shape the sound of rock music. Page is widely regarded as one of the greatest guitarists in history.

Here's a time line of Jimmy Page

  • 1944 - Born in Heston, England
  • 1960 - Becomes a session guitarist, playing for artists like The Kinks and The Who
  • 1966 - Joins The Yardbirds, replacing Jeff Beck as lead guitarist
  • 1968 - Forms Led Zeppelin with Robert Plant, John Paul Jones, and John Bonham
  • 1971 - Releases Led Zeppelin's debut album, marking the beginning of their iconic rock sound
  • 1980 - Writes "Stairway to Heaven," one of the most famous rock songs of all time
  • 1994 - Disbands Led Zeppelin following the death of drummer John Bonham
  • 2007 - Performs a one-off reunion concert with Led Zeppelin at the O2 Arena in London
